It is 25% of the year and so it is time to reflect on the studies that I have done in these four months. The hours of studying has decreased instead of increasing. It is maybe because it is harder to study maths than it is to study GS. I planned to give one mock test everyday for maths. But right now in the testbook app there is only 41 mocks. Based on the date of today it is roughly 1 mock every other day. I know it is going to increase as I have started to give 3 mocks everyday. (I am able to do it consistantly but yeah I am trying). One of the most igmored subject that I have to cover this year is Reasoning. I am getting pretty good marks in it nowadays. For past few mocks it has been consistantly over 40 (out of 50). I want to achieve full marks, as it is doable, but so far no luck. There is that one or two mistakes that keeps me away from scoring full marks. I am not giving the English Mock tests nowadays as I should spend more time with maths. I can finally say that the fear for maths has subsided a lot. The speed has increased. After attempting my first CGL Mains exam I had made a pact. I would not call her unless I get at least 80 percentile on Maths mock tests. I have improved my maths score a lot. I get on average 70-75 percentile, and if sometimes I am lucky or hyper-focused I touch the 90 percentile marks. Although the dream of getting full marks remains unachieveable. The highest I was able to score was 46/50. There is two or three topics that I have to revise again, those questions give me pain. These topics are Trigonometry, Plane Geometry, 2D mensuration, and last part of algebra. I am so glad I found out about Bhutesh sir. He might be the one to make me love maths.  

I do not know when, but GS has become the subject I do not want to study. The memorization makes me wanna fall asleep. But avoidance is not an option. I have not yet completed the last year's questions compilation. Polity is still gay. I am using anki flashcards to memorize it. [^1]
