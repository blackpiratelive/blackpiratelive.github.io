In this post I have wrriten down all the things that I have done for the exam preparations of SSC. I am talking about Staff Selection Commission of Government of India and **not the school service commission** of west bengal. 

### English
I did not study english from any coaching institute. My english is better than average so I just gave mocks. While giving mocks I realised that I am very bad at vocabulary. To fix this I wanted to complete all of the blackbook. But my strategy is bit different. 

I collected the previous year questions of all vocabulary pdfs. (You can get thsoe from telegram groups or whatsapp groups). Then I would copy all the text and ask any AI chatbots to generate anki flashcards for me. I asked them to generate a ```csv``` which then I would import to anki flashcards app. The exact prompt I used I do not remember but it is as follows. ```This text contains words and their meanings. I want to make anki flashcards from this. Make a csv with pipe seperator for easy import.``` You can modify this prompt to fit your needs. [I have talked about how I use AI in my studies before](/blog/how-i-use-ai-in-studies). 

After I was done with all the previous year vocabulary pdf that I can get, I then transformed all of Pinnacle 60 days vocabulary book to the anki flashcards. I think I have done only upto 100 pages and not all the pages, but it was enough. 

{{< img src="/img/pinnacle-book.png" >}}

It took me roughly 4-5 months to complete it. I was targeting one hour each day. Roughly 50-100 new vocabs I was doing with revisions of the old one. This is the best part of anki. It takes care of revision automatically. You can download this book [from here](/pdf/Pinnacle_SSC_60_Days_English_Vocabulary_3rd_Edition_20000+_Words.pdf). 

### Reasoning 
For reasoning I did not do much. I watched almost all the classes of VOD batch of Rankers Gurukul, RG Vikramjeet. The teacher is good but talks about offtopic stuff too much. Even in 2x speed it may feel like a waste of time. At the end of my reasoning syllabus I watched few topics from Piyush Varshny youtube channel. 


### Math
This is the most difficult subject for me. I started watching Gagan Pratap sir at the begining and completed 60-70% of the syllabus from him. Then I completed few topics namely, Profit and Loss, Boat and Stream, Time Speed and Distance from Abhinay Sir. I did compound interest from both Aditya Ranjan Sir and Gagan Pratap Sir. At the end I switched to Bhutesh Sir from e1 Coaching Centre.[^1] And I am sticking with him.

Of all the teachers I have watched for maths, I would recommended two. Gagan Pratap Sir if you are not from science or maths background, he starts from very basic and his videos are easy to understand. And secondly Bhutesh sir, if you are somewhat proficient in maths, or you have previously cleared CGL Pre and now stuck in Mains, Bhutesh Sir is best. You can watch his free one shot videos on his youtube channel. So far I have completed Algebra, Average from him and now continuing 2D Mensuration, 3D mensuration and Mixture and Alligation. In his [3D Mensuration video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gNWtqGktEas) he did only 2-3 pre questions and rest was from CGL Tier II, CDS, CAT etc. That is the level of Bhutesh sir. 

### General Studies and Current Affairs
For GS there is no teacher like Parmar SSC. I have completed his 3.0 batch, and watched few videos of 4.0 batch. I do not own the Fatman book and relied on his videos (both the batch and free content on youtube) for GS. If you are a beginner I suggest you start by watching his classes. I would not recommended just reading the notes or the fatman book. Watching his classes will make it easier to remember the facts. 

You can watch his PYQ series and FRB series, they are good for revisioning. I have also watched one shot videos of his. 

As for current affairs, I do monthly from parmar sir and compilation form him also. Currently looking to diversify my current affairs sources. 
[^1]: Bhutesh Sir has recently joined Parmar SSC
